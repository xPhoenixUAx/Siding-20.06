2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies can be:

  • Session cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser.
  • Persistent cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them.
  • First-party cookies: Cookies set by the website you are visiting.
  • Third-party cookies: Cookies set by a domain other than the one you are visiting.

Before you request a quote

Quick siding questions homeowners usually ask first.

Do I need repair or full siding replacement?

Repair can make sense when damage is limited to a small area and the surrounding wall is sound. Replacement is usually smarter when several walls are faded, brittle, warped, leaking, or repeatedly needing fixes.

What should be checked before new siding goes on?

The wall surface, sheathing, trim, window and door flashing, penetrations, roofline transitions, and weather barrier should be reviewed before new panels cover the exterior.

Can siding work include trim, soffit, fascia, and accents?

Yes. A full siding scope can include roofline details, window and door trim, corner boards, gable accents, board-and-batten sections, and other exterior details that affect the final look.

What information helps with a first estimate?

Useful details include the home address, photos of each exterior side, close-ups of problem areas, siding material preference if known, and whether the goal is repair, replacement, or curb appeal improvement.
